# AAV Gene Delivery

## Definition
Adeno-associated virus (AAV) vectors are the dominant platform for cardiac gene therapy. They are non-integrating (largely episomal), low-immunogenicity, replication-deficient viral vectors capable of transducing post-mitotic cardiomyocytes. AAV9 is the most commonly used serotype for cardiac gene delivery due to its cardiotropism.

## Key Concepts
- **Packaging capacity:** Standard AAV vectors accommodate ~4.7 kilobases of genetic payload — a critical constraint. This precludes direct replacement of large disease-causing genes: RYR2 (~15,000 nt) and SCN5A (~6,048 bp) both exceed this limit. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Workarounds for large genes:** Dual-vector systems (trans-splicing), mini-gene constructs — but these have inconsistent efficiency and unpredictable expression dynamics. Pathway-targeted gene therapy (e.g., MOG1 for BrS, CASQ2 for CPVT1) bypasses size constraints by targeting shared molecular nodes. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **AAV9 in cardiac studies:** Used successfully for CASQ2 replacement (CPVT), SupRep for LQTS, PKP2 replacement (ARVC), CaMKII peptide inhibitor delivery, base editing for LQT3, CRISPR-SaCas9 for CPVT1. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Routes of administration:**
  - *Intravenous:* Least invasive; significant off-target liver accumulation; lower cardiac transduction efficiency at high doses needed.
  - *Intracoronary:* Catheter-delivered; better cardiac uptake; less effective with coronary disease.
  - *Intramyocardial:* Targeted regional delivery; used in surgery; invasive; limited spread; risk of localized inflammation/arrhythmias. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Immune responses:** Pre-existing or treatment-induced anti-AAV antibodies can limit efficacy and prevent re-dosing. Mitigations under development: transient immunosuppression, capsid re-engineering, extracellular vesicle shielding. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Integration risk:** AAV is primarily episomal, but rare genomic integration has been reported — theoretical insertional mutagenesis risk requiring long-term monitoring. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Non-viral alternatives:** Lipid nanoparticles (LNPs) encapsulating mRNA provide transient, non-integrating expression with low immunogenicity. Demonstrated safety in EPICCURE trial (VEGF-A mRNA). TBX18 mRNA (naked, unformulated) demonstrated biological pacing in rats and pigs. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Future directions:** Next-generation cardiotropic AAV capsids with enhanced specificity; antibody-oligonucleotide conjugates; image-guided/magnetically guided delivery systems. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **IV vs. intracoronary route — efficacy vs. feasibility tradeoff:** Intravenous delivery offers the lowest procedural risk but produces significant off-target liver transduction and lower cardiac uptake. Intracoronary delivery improves cardiac specificity but requires cath lab access and is less effective in the presence of coronary disease. No head-to-head RCT defines the optimal route for any cardiac gene therapy indication; route choice is currently extrapolated from animal studies and manufacturing constraints. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Re-dosing prevented by immune responses:** Anti-AAV neutralizing antibodies develop after first treatment, precluding re-dosing with the same serotype. For chronic cardiac diseases (HCM, LMNA cardiomyopathy), where durability beyond 5–10 years is required, the single-dose constraint is a major unresolved barrier. Capsid re-engineering and extracellular vesicle shielding are proposed but unvalidated in humans. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Episomal dilution in pediatric patients:** AAV integrates rarely and remains primarily episomal. In growing pediatric patients, cardiomyocyte expansion dilutes episomal transgene copies over time. The durability of any AAV-based cardiac therapy delivered in childhood is uncertain and may require re-treatment strategies that pre-existing immunity currently prevents. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
