# Clonal Hematopoiesis of Indeterminate Potential (CHIP)

## Definition
Clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential (CHIP) is defined as the presence of an **expanded somatic blood cell clone** harbouring a driver mutation in individuals without other haematological abnormalities (no haematological cancer diagnosis, normal blood counts). CHIP is common in older populations and represents a convergence point between oncology and cardiovascular medicine — it is associated with **both** hematologic malignancy risk **and** increased cardiovascular events.

## Key Concepts

### Epidemiology
- CHIP is **common in older populations** (prevalence increases substantially after age 60–70). ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]], rating: very high)
- The three most frequently mutated driver genes are **DNMT3A**, **ASXL1**, and **TET2** — each found in expanded blood cell clones. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])

### CHIP as a Dual Cancer and CVD Risk Factor
- **Hematologic malignancy:** CHIP confers increased risk of haematological cancer (the expanded clone can progress to overt malignancy). ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- **Cardiovascular disease:** CHIP is also a risk factor for **MI, stroke, and all-cause mortality** in older populations — independent of traditional CVD risk factors (Jaiswal et al., NEJM 2014 discovery cohort). ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- This dual association makes CHIP a paradigm for shared cancer-CVD risk.

### Implicated Genes and CVD Mechanisms
- **DNMT3A, ASXL1, TET2:** Each gene mutation individually associated with **coronary heart disease**. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- **Atherosclerosis pathogenesis:** Basic science models suggest these genes participate in the pathogenesis of atherosclerosis — potentially via altered macrophage/monocyte function in the vessel wall (inflammatory signalling in haematopoietic cells). ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- The mechanism is thought to involve clonal expansion of inflammatory macrophage precursors that infiltrate the atherosclerotic plaque and amplify vascular inflammation.

### Implications for the Cancer-CVD Interface
- CHIP illustrates that the elevated CVD risk in cancer patients/survivors is not solely due to cardiotoxic treatments — **shared genetic predisposition** also contributes. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- Identifying CHIP may help stratify cardiovascular risk in cancer populations — a potential future tool for precision cardio-oncology.
- The >16 million American cancer survivors face risk from both cancer recurrence and CVD; CHIP-related genetic risk may be relevant to both. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- CANTOS trial analogy: IL-1β inhibition reduced both cardiac events and lung cancer incidence, supporting common inflammatory mechanisms underlying both conditions. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])

### Other Shared Cancer-CVD Risk Factors
- **27-Hydroxycholesterol:** Cholesterol metabolite that acts as a direct estrogen receptor agonist in breast cancer cells, stimulating tumour growth; epidemiological association between dietary cholesterol and breast cancer risk (48% increase in top vs. bottom quartile).
- **IL-1β/inflammation:** CANTOS trial — canakinumab (anti-IL-1β) significantly reduced both MACE and lung cancer incidence/mortality.
- **ARIC study:** Adherence to all 7 AHA 2020 cardiovascular health metrics inversely associated with cancer (strongest for breast, colorectal, lung) AND CVD — shared modifiable risk factors.

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- The mechanisms by which DNMT3A/ASXL1/TET2 mutations drive atherosclerosis are not fully elucidated — macrophage-centric hypothesis is leading but not proven. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- It is unclear whether treating CHIP (removing the clone or targeting its inflammatory output) would reduce CVD risk — a major research question. ([[sources/cardio-oncology-vascular-metabolic-aha-2019]])
- Whether CHIP should be routinely tested in high-risk cardio-oncology patients, and whether it should modify management, is undefined.
- The CANTOS IL-1β connection provides indirect support but causality between CHIP-driven inflammation and atherosclerosis requires direct demonstration.
