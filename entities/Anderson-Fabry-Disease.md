# Anderson-Fabry Disease (AFD)

## Details
Anderson-Fabry disease (AFD) is an X-linked lysosomal storage disorder caused by variants in the GLA gene encoding alpha-galactosidase A (α-Gal A), leading to progressive accumulation of globotriaosylceramide (Gb3) in multiple organs including the myocardium, kidneys, peripheral nerves, and skin. Cardiac involvement typically manifests as a phenocopy of HCM (LVH) with unique features that should prompt specific investigation. It is found in ~0.9% of patients presenting with LVH in selected screening programs.

## Key Facts

### Diagnosis and Red Flags
- Diagnosis requires α-Gal A enzyme activity (males; reduced in affected males) + GLA genetic analysis (females; enzyme activity may be normal in heterozygous females). ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **Cardiac CMR red flags:** Basal-inferolateral/posterolateral LGE + low native T1 values (pathognomonic combination — low native T1 reflects lipid accumulation, not fibrosis). ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **Echocardiographic red flags:** Concentric LVH with normal systolic function, hypertrophy of papillary muscles, AV valve thickening, reduced global longitudinal strain. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **ECG red flags:** Short PQ interval in young patients (glycolipid infiltration of conduction tissue); AV blocks in adults; LVH; bradycardia. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **Extracardiac red flags:** Neuropathic pain, angiokeratomas, cornea verticillata, albuminuria/renal involvement, hypohidrosis, GI symptoms, hearing loss, no male-to-male transmission in pedigree. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- Biopsy (endomyocardial or renal/skin) should include electron microscopy to detect lamellar bodies. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- Plasma lyso-Gb3 provides additional diagnostic information when GLA variant pathogenicity is uncertain. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])

### Management
- **Enzyme replacement therapy (ERT):** Agalsidase alfa or agalsidase beta — indicated in all symptomatic patients with classical disease at earliest signs of organ involvement. Early initiation is important as ERT has limited efficacy in advanced disease with irreversible organ damage. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- Pharmacological chaperone (migalastat): for patients with amenable GLA mutations — orally active, may be preferred in eligible patients.
- Second-generation ERTs, substrate-reduction therapies, and gene/mRNA therapies are in development. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- Cardiac manifestations managed as per standard HCM/HF guidelines, with key exception: mavacamten and HCM Risk-SCD should NOT be used in AFD — these are HCM genocopies with distinct management. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **ERT efficacy — optimal initiation timing undefined:** ERT (agalsidase alfa/beta) is recommended at the earliest signs of organ involvement, as it has limited efficacy in advanced disease with irreversible damage. However, no validated staging system precisely defines when irreversibility begins, and the staging criteria for initiating ERT vary between guidelines. The clinical consequence is heterogeneous prescribing practice with uncertain impact on outcomes when treatment is initiated in intermediate-stage disease. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **Low native T1 on CMR — underrecognized outside expert centres:** The combination of basal-inferolateral LGE + low native T1 values is pathognomonic for AFD on CMR. However, native T1 mapping requires dedicated sequences and calibration to reference ranges; in centres without T1 mapping expertise, this characteristic pattern is missed, contributing to diagnostic delay. Most CMR referrals at non-specialist centres may not include systematic native T1 assessment in LVH patients. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
- **AFD presenting as HCM phenocopy — mavacamten and HCM Risk-SCD misuse risk:** AFD is explicitly identified as an HCM genocopy in which HCM-specific tools (mavacamten for LVOTO, HCM Risk-SCD calculator) are NOT recommended. With ~0.9% of LVH patients having AFD in screening programs, a proportion of patients receiving HCM-directed therapies may have undiagnosed AFD. The pre-treatment alpha-galactosidase screen is an underimplemented clinical safeguard. ([[sources/esc-cmp-2023]])
