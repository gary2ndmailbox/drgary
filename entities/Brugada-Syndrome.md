# Brugada Syndrome (BrS)

## Details
Brugada syndrome is an inherited channelopathy characterized by a distinctive ECG pattern and predisposition to fatal ventricular arrhythmias in the absence of structural heart disease. It is a major cause of SCD in young individuals with structurally normal hearts, accounting for 4–12% of all SCD cases and up to 28% of SCDs in structurally normal hearts.

## Key Facts
- Prevalence: ~3–5 per 10,000; 8–10× more common in males. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- ~2/3 of patients are asymptomatic; 1/3 present with syncope, palpitations, or dizziness; SCD can be the first manifestation. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Arrhythmias and SCD tend to occur at rest, during sleep, or after large meals (vagal tone/bradycardia periods). ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Associated conduction abnormalities: 1st-degree AV block, RBBB, intraventricular conduction delay, sick sinus syndrome. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Genetics:** SCN5A (loss-of-function, AD) is the **only ClinGen-curated core gene** for BrS, implicated in 20–30% of cases and associated with more severe phenotype including conduction disease. A 2019 study (Campuzano et al.) additionally identified SLMAP, SEMA3A, SCNN1A, and SCN2B as BrS-related disease genes. Other genes (SCN10A, CACNA1C, CACNB2, CACNA2D1, KCND3, KCNJ8) have been reported but lack strong ClinGen validation. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]], [[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **Pathophysiology (controversial):** Three non-mutually exclusive hypotheses: (1) Repolarization: reduced inward currents or increased Ito creates outward current shift in RV epicardial AP → phase 2 reentry; (2) Depolarization: fibrosis and reduced Nav/connexin-43 in RV outflow tract slows conduction; (3) Neural crest: abnormal connexin-43 expression from cardiac neural crest cells causes conduction delay. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **ECG types:** Type 1 (coved): J-point ≥2 mm, descending ST, negative T in V1/V2 — the diagnostic pattern. Type 2 (saddleback): J-point ≥2 mm, terminal ST ≥1 mm, positive/biphasic T. Type 3: saddleback, terminal ST <1 mm. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Diagnosis:** ESC Class I: spontaneous type 1 ECG without structural disease, OR inducible type 1 ECG (drug/fever) in a patient with SCA or VF. Drug provocation (ajmaline, flecainide, pilsicainide, procainamide) unmasks type 1 in types 2/3; ajmaline preferred. **Shanghai Diagnostic Score:** ≥3.5 = definitive BrS; 2–3 = possible/suspected. Incorporates ECG type, family history, syncope, and genetic findings to account for phenocopies (mechanical RVOT compression, ischaemia, electrolyte disturbances, hyperthermia, drug intoxications). ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]], [[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **Genetic testing — Class I recommendation** per EHRA/HRS/APHRS/LAHRS consensus when spontaneous type 1 ECG is present; Class IIb when only type 2/3 ECG. SCN5A variant finding may contribute to future risk stratification alongside PRS from GWAS loci. ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **Risk stratification:** Spontaneous type 1, history of syncope, and documented VA are strongest predictors. EPS controversial but useful for intermediate-risk patients. Drug-induced type 1 → lower risk, managed conservatively. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Management:** ICD for symptomatic/high-risk patients. Quinidine/hydroquinidine for ICD-contraindicated or ICD-declined patients. IV isoproterenol for electrical storms. Radiofrequency ablation for recurrent ICD shocks or refractory cases — can normalize ECG and eliminate VT/VF inducibility. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Emerging:** BrAID trial (NCT04641585): ML + transcriptomics platform for type 1 BrS diagnosis. RNA-based and noncoding RNA therapeutics under investigation. ARumenamide-787 (inhibits Ito, enhances INa) showed promising anti-arrhythmic effects in canine BrS/ERS models. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Direct SCN5A gene replacement is infeasible — coding sequence ~6,048 bp approaches/exceeds standard AAV packaging capacity (~4.7 kb). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **MOG1 overexpression strategy:** AAV9-mediated MOG1 (Ran guanine nucleotide release factor, Nav1.5 chaperone) delivery enhanced Nav1.5 cell-surface trafficking and reversed the arrhythmic phenotype in SCN5A-G1746R knock-in mice — pathway-targeted workaround to the SCN5A size constraint (Yu 2022). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])

- **ESC 2022 guideline recommendations:**
  - Diagnose BrS with spontaneous type 1 ECG + no structural disease: **Class I**. Diagnose if SCA survivor with VF/PVT + type 1 pattern induced by sodium channel blocker or fever: **Class I**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - SCN5A genetic testing for probands: **Class I**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - Avoid BrS-aggravating drugs (brugadadrugs.org), fever, cocaine, cannabis, excess alcohol: **Class I**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - ICD for aborted CA or documented sustained VT: **Class I**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - ILR for BrS + unexplained syncope: **Class IIa**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - Catheter ablation of triggering PVCs/RVOT epicardial substrate for recurrent ICD shocks refractory to drug therapy: **Class IIa** (upgraded from IIb). ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - PES may be considered in asymptomatic patients with spontaneous type 1 BrS ECG: **Class IIb**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - Sodium channel blocker test if prior type 1 pattern: **NOT recommended (Class III)**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
  - Catheter ablation in asymptomatic BrS patients: **NOT recommended (Class III)**. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **Catheter ablation asymmetry — symptomatic vs. asymptomatic BrS:** ESC 2022 upgraded catheter ablation for recurrent ICD shocks/refractory drug therapy to Class IIa (from IIb), recognizing its efficacy in eliminating epicardial RVOT substrate. Yet ablation in asymptomatic BrS is simultaneously Class III. This creates a sharp binary that may not reflect the clinical reality of intermediate-risk patients — e.g., those with spontaneous type 1 ECG and family history of SCD but no personal arrhythmic event. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
- **Unresolved pathophysiology:** Three non-mutually exclusive mechanistic hypotheses (repolarization, depolarization, neural crest) remain unreconciled — this directly affects the rationale for ablation (targeting depolarization substrate) versus drug therapy (targeting repolarization/Ito). Any guideline recommendation for ablation implicitly assumes the depolarization/conduction hypothesis without fully resolving the debate. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **PES role:** PES remains Class IIb (may be considered) in asymptomatic patients with spontaneous type 1 BrS ECG — controversial because EPS has limited negative predictive value and its use to identify candidates for ablation in asymptomatic patients conflicts with the Class III recommendation against ablation in this group. ([[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]])
- **SCN5A testing yield:** SCN5A is causative in only 20–30% of BrS probands — a Class I recommendation for testing yields a positive result in the minority, and a negative result does not exclude the diagnosis. Despite being the sole ClinGen-curated core gene, the majority of BrS patients remain genotype-negative after SCN5A testing. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]], [[sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022]], [[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
