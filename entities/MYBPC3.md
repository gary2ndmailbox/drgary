# MYBPC3 (Cardiac Myosin Binding Protein C)

## Details
MYBPC3 encodes cardiac myosin binding protein C (cMyBP-C), a thick-filament–associated regulatory protein of the sarcomere. MYBPC3 mutations are the single most common genetic cause of hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), accounting for up to 50% of all identified pathogenic variants. Mutations span the entire gene and are largely heterozygous truncating variants producing haploinsufficiency, though missense variants also occur.

## Key Facts
- **Most common HCM gene:** MYBPC3 mutations account for ~50% of identified HCM-causing variants; MYH7 is second (~25–40%). ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Arrhythmia risk:** Japanese HCM patient studies found MYBPC3 mutation carriers have a higher incidence of ventricular arrhythmia and syncope compared to other sarcomere mutation carriers. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Knockin mouse model (G>A transition, exon 6):** Exhibits higher myofilament Ca²⁺ sensitivity, faster Ca²⁺ transient decay, and diastolic dysfunction; confirmed proarrhythmic substrate. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **K⁺ current controversy:** Mybpc3-KI mice show reduced K⁺ currents as the dominant proarrhythmic mechanism — but this finding was NOT replicated in human MYBPC3-homozygous EHT nor in LV septum samples from HCM patients, illustrating the species translational gap. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **RyR2 interaction:** MYBPC3 may interact directly with RyR2; MYBPC3 mutation or conformational change can disrupt intracellular Ca²⁺ homeostasis through this protein–protein interaction. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Preclinical HCM:** MYBPC3 is a primary target in gene screening for identifying genotype-positive/phenotype-negative relatives; its incomplete penetrance and variable expressivity complicate genotype-phenotype correlation. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- MYBPC3 is listed alongside ACTC1, TPM1 as encoding thin-filament components indirectly; structurally it is a thick-filament regulatory protein. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Atrial fibrosis substrate in AF ablation:** MYBPC3 variant–positive HCM patients undergoing AF ablation have significantly more LA low-voltage areas than gene-negative HCM controls (normal voltage 85.6% vs. 94.3%; intermediate scar 7.12% vs. 3.07%; dense scar 7.31% vs. 2.61%), despite similar LA pressures — supporting primary atrial myopathy driven by the genetic variant, independent of hemodynamic loading. ([[sources/MYBPC3-MYH7-JACCEP-2024]], rating: medium)
- **Greater LV mass in MYBPC3 vs. MYH7 carriers:** MYBPC3 variant–positive patients had significantly greater LV mass index (145.7 ± 48.4 g/m² vs. 86.6 ± 27.3 g/m² in MYH7; P=0.04); this may contribute to thicker LA walls and higher rates of non-transmural ablation lesions → pulmonary vein reconnection. ([[sources/MYBPC3-MYH7-JACCEP-2024]])
- **AF ablation outcomes:** Freedom from AF at 12 months in MYBPC3 variant–positive HCM (66.7%) was similar to controls (73.3%; P=0.92) but required more procedures on average; all gene-positive patients remained on AADs post-ablation. ([[sources/MYBPC3-MYH7-JACCEP-2024]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **K⁺ current reduction — mouse finding not replicated in human:** Mybpc3 knock-in mice show reduced K⁺ currents as the dominant proarrhythmic mechanism for HCM arrhythmogenesis. This finding was NOT replicated in human MYBPC3-homozygous engineered heart tissue (EHT) nor in LV septum samples from HCM patients undergoing surgical myectomy. The translational gap undermines using mouse MYBPC3 models to guide antiarrhythmic drug development for human HCM. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Incomplete penetrance — genotype-positive/phenotype-negative relatives uncoupled from ICD decisions:** MYBPC3 mutations show variable expressivity and incomplete penetrance. A genotype-positive relative of an HCM proband may never develop phenotypic disease. Current guidelines recommend surveillance but not pre-emptive ICD. The lack of penetrance predictors means genotype-positive individuals carry a labelled diagnosis with uncertain individual risk — with implications for insurance, employment, and psychological wellbeing. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **Haploinsufficiency vs. dominant-negative mechanism — therapeutically relevant distinction:** Most MYBPC3 pathogenic variants are truncating and produce haploinsufficiency (reduced functional protein level). Some may produce a dominant-negative poison peptide. These mechanisms have different therapeutic implications — gene replacement would address haploinsufficiency; gene silencing would be needed for dominant-negative. Distinguishing the mechanism for individual variants is not routinely done in clinical practice. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
