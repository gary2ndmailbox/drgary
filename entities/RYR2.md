# RYR2 (Ryanodine Receptor 2)

## Details
RYR2 is the gene encoding ryanodine receptor 2 (RyR2), the primary sarcoplasmic reticulum Ca²⁺ release channel in cardiomyocytes. Located on chromosome 1q42-q43, gain-of-function mutations in RYR2 are the predominant genetic cause of catecholaminergic polymorphic ventricular tachycardia (CPVT1), accounting for 60–70% of all CPVT cases.

## Key Facts
- **Mechanism:** Gain-of-function RYR2 mutations cause hyperactive Ca²⁺ release channels. During adrenergic stimulation (exercise/emotion), excessive Ca²⁺ accumulates in the sarcoplasmic reticulum, followed by spontaneous diastolic Ca²⁺ release → delayed afterdepolarizations → triggered ventricular arrhythmias. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Mutations are typically missense variants producing autosomal dominant inheritance. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- RYR2 forms the calcium release unit alongside calsequestrin-2 (CASQ2), juntin (ASPH), and triadin (TRDN). CASQ2 is the main Ca²⁺ buffer and modulates RYR2 activity; CASQ2 mutations indirectly destabilize the RYR2-mediated release unit. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- RYR2 is also listed among genes associated with IVF (as RyR2-encoded cardiac Ca²⁺ channel). ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Ca²⁺ Release Deficiency Syndrome (CRDS):** Distinct from CPVT, CRDS is caused by RYR2 **loss-of-function** variants — producing reduced Ca²⁺ release rather than the excessive release seen in CPVT1. CRDS presents with short-coupled ventricular torsade-de-pointes arrhythmias (not exercise-induced). RYR2 exon 3 deletion has been associated with LVNC and atypical CPVT overlap. CRDS has been formally established as a novel RYR2-related phenotypic spectrum (Steinberg et al., Europace 2023). ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- RYR2 gain-of-function accounts for ~50–60% of CPVT cases per ClinGen-informed estimates; CASQ2 (~5%, AR) is second; ClinGen definitive CPVT genes also include TECRL and TRDN. ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **Therapeutic target:** CRISPR/Cas9 silencing of mutant RYR2 alleles is under exploration. Dantrolene (a RYR1 blocker used for malignant hyperthermia) has theoretical relevance to RYR2 but no human CPVT trial data exist. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Over 150 distinct pathogenic RYR2 variants have been identified, all promoting aberrant diastolic Ca²⁺ leakage → delayed afterdepolarizations. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- Direct gene replacement is infeasible: RYR2 coding sequence ~15,000 nt far exceeds AAV packaging capacity (~4.7 kb). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- CRISPR-SaCas9 (AAV9) targeting RYR2-R4496C achieved ~41% editing efficiency in cardiomyocytes; 0/7 treated vs. 7/8 control mice had arrhythmias — first in-vivo demonstration of safe CRISPR-mediated RYR2 repair (Pan 2023). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- Allele-specific silencing (siRNA/shRNA) suppressed mutant Ryr2 in mice; also restored junctional SR and T-tubular ultrastructure. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- Pathway-targeted alternatives bypass RYR2 size constraints: CASQ2 overexpression suppresses arrhythmias in RYR2-CPVT1; CaMKII inhibitory peptide (AIP) via AAV9 normalizes Ca²⁺ handling; modified calmodulin delivery increases RYR2 refractoriness. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- The DPP6 haplotype biomarker elevated in IVF is distinct from RYR2 pathways; IVF and CPVT share phenotypic overlap but differ in arrhythmia trigger (R-on-T PVCs vs. exercise-induced delayed afterdepolarizations). ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])

- **VUS burden in early-onset AF:** RYR2 was among the most common VUS genes (66 VUSs) in 1293 patients with early-onset AF who underwent WGS. Because RYR2 gain-of-function is the primary cause of CPVT (potentially fatal exercise-triggered VT), detection of a RYR2 VUS requires anticipatory genetic counseling to contextualize uncertainty and avoid inappropriate clinical anxiety. ([[sources/eoaf-jama-2021]])
- **RyR2 in HCM:** RyR2 hyperphosphorylation at CaMKII-sensitive sites (secondary to cytosolic Ca²⁺ accumulation from sarcomere mutations) → ↑RyR2 opening probability → spontaneous SR Ca²⁺ release → EADs/DADs → ventricular arrhythmia. Confirmed in human HCM specimens and mouse models. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **MYBPC3-RyR2 interaction:** RyR2 may interact directly with MYBPC3; MYBPC3 mutation or conformational change disrupts this interaction → altered intracellular Ca²⁺ homeostasis. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
- **RyR2-P1124L in genotype-negative HCM:** This RyR2 mutation increases RyR2 Ca²⁺ sensitivity → arrhythmia + secondary structural cardiac remodeling in patients who otherwise appear genotype-negative for classical HCM sarcomere genes. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **CRISPR-SaCas9 41% editing efficiency — sufficient preclinically, untested in humans:** Pan 2023 demonstrated 41% cardiomyocyte editing efficiency with CRISPR-SaCas9 targeting RYR2-R4496C and 0/7 treated vs. 7/8 control mice had arrhythmias. The editing efficiency threshold for arrhythmia prevention in humans is unknown. Off-target editing safety in non-dividing human cardiomyocytes over decades has not been assessed. The mouse result establishes proof-of-concept but cannot be directly extrapolated to predict human therapeutic safety or efficacy. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **Dantrolene — theoretical RYR2 relevance without human CPVT trial data:** Dantrolene inhibits RYR1 (skeletal muscle) and is used for malignant hyperthermia. It has theoretical applicability to RYR2-mediated CPVT given structural homology between RYR1 and RYR2. However, no human CPVT trial data for dantrolene exist, and its cardiac side effects (negative inotropy, hypotension) limit clinical feasibility in symptomatic CPVT. This remains a hypothesis not validated in human practice. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **CRDS vs. CPVT — loss-of-function RYR2 is a distinct syndrome:** The identification of CRDS as a separate RYR2 phenotype means that RYR2 variants now span a gain-of-function (CPVT) and loss-of-function (CRDS) spectrum — analogous to SCN5A causing LQT3 (GOF) vs. BrS (LOF). The same gene can produce opposite arrhythmia triggers (exercise-induced in CPVT; short-coupled rest arrhythmias in CRDS), with likely different optimal treatments. Functional characterization of RYR2 variant direction is clinically essential. ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **RYR2-P1124L in "genotype-negative HCM" — category blurring:** The RYR2-P1124L variant causes increased Ca²⁺ sensitivity, arrhythmia, and secondary structural cardiac remodeling resembling HCM in patients who are negative for classical sarcomere gene mutations. This blurs the boundary between CPVT and HCM, raising the question of whether some "genotype-negative HCM" patients actually have a calcium-channel arrhythmogenic disease misclassified as a sarcomeric cardiomyopathy — with management implications if HCM-specific risk calculators are applied to this group. ([[sources/HCM-VA-FCVMed-2022]])
