# SCN5A

## Details
SCN5A is the gene encoding Nav1.5, the α-subunit of the primary cardiac voltage-gated sodium channel. It is located on chromosome 3p21-p24 and is the single most important gene in cardiac channelopathies, capable of producing distinct syndromes depending on whether the mutation is gain- or loss-of-function.

## Key Facts
- **LQT3 (gain-of-function):** Persistent Na⁺ influx from phase 0 into the plateau of the action potential, prolonging repolarization. Accounts for 5–10% of LQTS cases. Mexiletine has mutation-specific benefit in LQT3. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **Brugada syndrome (loss-of-function):** Reduced INa → slower conduction in RV outflow tract → depolarization heterogeneity → arrhythmias. SCN5A loss-of-function mutations account for 15–30% of BrS cases and are associated with a more severe disease phenotype. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- SCN5A is also implicated in ERS (loss-of-function, rare), CPVT (rare), and through functional overlap with other sodium channel subunit genes (SCN1B, SCN2B, SCN3B, SCN4B, SCN10A) which modify Nav1.5 activity. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **SCN5A phenotype breadth:** A single gene producing distinct syndromes depending on variant effect direction — LOF: Brugada syndrome, cardiac conduction disease (CCD/PCCD), dilated cardiomyopathy; GOF: LQT3. SCN5A is also listed among genes for familial atrial fibrillation and short-coupled TdP (scTdP), multifocal ectopic Purkinje-related premature contractions (MEPPC), sinus node disease, and cardiac conduction disease (syndromal and isolated forms). ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- **Cardiac conduction disease (CCD/PCCD):** SCN5A (along with TRPM4 and GJA5, SCN1B) is a core gene for isolated CCD; syndromal CCD involves LMNA, TNNI3K, DES, DMD, DMPK, and others. Variant detection rate for CCD is 10–20%. ([[sources/arrhythmia-genetics-mgenetik-2025]])
- SCN5A loss-of-function in BrS is associated with additional conduction disease: 1st-degree AV block, RBBB, sick sinus syndrome. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- Mutations in SCN5A can alter channel gating, trafficking, and drug-binding sites, affecting pharmacological responses and supporting the rationale for genotype-guided therapy. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- SCN5A coding sequence ~6,048 bp approaches/exceeds standard AAV packaging capacity (~4.7 kb) — direct replacement is infeasible with current vectors. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **BrS gene therapy workaround:** AAV9-mediated MOG1 overexpression enhances Nav1.5 trafficking to the cell surface, compensating for trafficking-deficient SCN5A loss-of-function mutations. Reversed arrhythmic phenotype in SCN5A-G1746R knock-in mice (Yu 2022). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **LQT3 base editing:** Adenine base editor ABE8e-SpRY split across dual AAV9 corrected SCN5A-M1875T (T1307M) in mice: 54% editing efficiency, QTc normalized, late INa reduced 66%. Notably, ~20% editing was sufficient to prevent arrhythmias — explained by source-sink electrotonic coupling through gap junctions. ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])

- **ACM role:** Pathogenic SCN5A variants cause arrhythmogenic forms of DCM and LVNC (~2% of ACM; up to 5–10% when combined with LMNA variants in DCM with progressive conduction defects). Phenotypes include atrial standstill, progressive AV block, AF, sick sinus syndrome, VT, torsades de pointes, VF, and arrhythmic sudden death. ([[sources/acm-hrs-2019]])
- **Functional link to desmosome:** Nav1.5 co-precipitates with the desmosomal protein PKP2 and the AJ protein N-cadherin (CDH2). Primary desmosomal mutations (PKP2) reduce INa amplitude and kinetics; conversely, primary SCN5A mutations can affect N-cadherin biology and reduce N-cadherin cluster size and density at the ID. ([[sources/acm-hrs-2019]])
- SCN5A loss-of-function (as in Brugada syndrome) is also a recognized but uncommon ACM gene variant (~2% of ACM cases), producing a phenotype of arrhythmogenic DCM with broad ECG abnormalities. ([[sources/acm-hrs-2019]])

## Contradictions / Open Questions
- **SCN5A bidirectional phenotype — opposite syndromes, opposite drug responses, same gene:** SCN5A gain-of-function (LQT3) requires late Na⁺ channel blockade (mexiletine); SCN5A loss-of-function (Brugada syndrome) is worsened by sodium channel blockers (flecainide, procainamide — which are both used as diagnostic provocateurs in BrS). A patient with a novel SCN5A variant of uncertain significance who is prescribed a sodium channel blocker for another indication could be harmed if the variant is loss-of-function. Functional characterization of SCN5A variant direction of effect is critical before pharmacological therapy decisions. ([[sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025]])
- **SCN5A coding sequence exceeds AAV packaging capacity — direct gene replacement infeasible:** At ~6,048 bp, SCN5A coding sequence approaches or exceeds the standard AAV packaging limit (~4,700 bp), making full-length SCN5A replacement via AAV infeasible with current vectors. The MOG1 trafficking workaround and LQT3 base editing approach are both workarounds for this fundamental constraint, each with different mechanistic assumptions and limited by their narrow applicability (MOG1 only addresses trafficking-deficient variants; base editing only addresses specific base substitutions). ([[sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025]])
- **SCN5A loss-of-function in ACM — additional phenotypic complexity:** SCN5A loss-of-function accounts for ~2% of ACM cases (up to 5–10% with LMNA overlap) and produces arrhythmogenic DCM with broad ECG abnormalities. This phenotypic overlap with desmosomal ACM means that SCN5A carriers may be initially evaluated with ARVC task force criteria, which were not designed for this genotype. The sodium channelopathy phenotype in this group carries management implications beyond standard ACM (e.g., avoid sodium channel blockers). ([[sources/acm-hrs-2019]])
